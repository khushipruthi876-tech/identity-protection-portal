💡 Awareness & Tips

Practical steps you can take today to reduce your risk of identity theft and account compromise.

🔑 Use a Password Manager

Reusing passwords across sites means one breach can compromise every account tied to that password. A password manager generates and stores a unique, strong password for every site automatically.

🔐 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds a second layer of proof beyond your password — usually a code from your phone. Even if your password leaks, an attacker still can't get in without that second factor.

📧 Watch for Phishing Emails

Be suspicious of urgent emails asking you to "verify your account" or "reset your password" through a link. Always check the sender's actual email address and hover over links before clicking.

🕵️ Limit What You Share Publicly

Information like your birthday, pet's name, or mother's maiden name is often used in security questions. Avoid sharing these details publicly on social media.

🔄 Check for Breaches Regularly

Use this portal's Breach Check tool periodically — not just once — since new breaches are discovered constantly, and your information could be exposed at any time.

🧹 Review Your Digital Footprint

Old, forgotten accounts are a common attack surface. Use the Footprint Analyzer to see where your username shows up, and delete or secure accounts you no longer use.
